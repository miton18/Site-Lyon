The history of Lyon in brief

Lyon and its region offer an outstanding quality of life. The inhabitants and the tourists appreciate This city of art and culture, through its history and its very rich holdings, its numerous events Cultural and artistic, his magnificent spaces of leisure activities, as the famous Park of the Golden head and The remarkable arrangement of the banks of the Rhône. Without forgetting the closeness with the nature, the region of Beaujolais, The mountains of the Alps, the summit of Europe, and the biggest domains of ski of the world.

In summary: Lyon was established in 43 by Romain, but the site was occupied by the Gauls since 600 approximately. She develops Quickly and becomes the capital of the Gaul in 27. In its peak, in the second century, the city has an important role in the Empire And establish the pride of the emperors, which stays there regularly. She declines at the same time as Roman Empire.

Lugdunum in 2th century.


It is Charlemagne who is going to return to Lyon a little of its vitality of former days. But it is during the Renaissance that Lyon is going to know sound Second golden age. It becomes the economic capital of France and financial capital of Europe. It becomes a big Cultural center. François 1st intends even to transfer it his capital.

Lyon in 1550.


During the Revolution, the Jacobins of Paris are going to kill thousands of inhabitants of Lyon and to try to line Lyon of the map. But Napoleon Bonaparte will cross by Lyon, will rebuild her and will return him its chandelier of former days by doping the industry of the silk, Local speciality. Napoleon is bound of passion for Lyon and his inhabitants. He too intends to transfer it his capital.

Ronald Zins's book describes well the links which unite Lyon and Napoleon.


During the Second World War, Lyon is the center of the Resistance. Charles de Gaulle qualified her as " capital of the resistance ". Today, Lyon continues its ascent towards a likely new golden age.

Lyon continues its ascent and aspires to compete with the big European metropolises.

The city can boast about several geographical specificities: situated halfway enter sea And mountain, it is overhung by two hills Fourvière and Croix-Rousse and crossed by two Stream the Rhône and its tributary the Saône, which join even in Lyon. This exceptional situation In the confluence of both streams, confer on the city its physical peculiarity: his peninsula.
A major urban project concerns the south extremity of this peninsula, for a long time dedicated to the industry and in Transport: the Lyon Confluence project. The objective is to reveal the beauty of the river landscapes and To open up this territory to create a new district, where live offices, businesses and houses, All gather in an exemplary project regarding sustainable development.

In front of world climatic stakes, the question of the mobility became a major concern for metropolises. Real alternatives in the individual car, the fashions soft bikes, rollers, pedestrians and scooters are not motorized 100 % and Non-polluting. In narrow link with the collective transport bus, streetcar, train, subway, the ride sharing, the car sharing, these new Ways of transportation replace the man in the center of the public place.
